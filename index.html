About:

The Himalayas is a mountain range in South Asia stretching across the Tibetan Plateau across Bhutan, Tibet, Nepal, and Northeast India.

The Himalayas are the largest Mountain Range in Asia and are known for its jagged peaks and its many different zones of ecology. Avalanches occur often which help build new ecosystems in different ranges and complex geological structure. Another main characteristic is the climate because it recieves the strong monsson winds causing a cool climate compared to other mountain regions. In addition, the Himalayas have one of the most diverse ecosystem due to its cool forests and alpine climates.

The Himalayas are part of the Alpine Biome as they are a mountain range and so with high elevation comes cooler climates and strong winds.
